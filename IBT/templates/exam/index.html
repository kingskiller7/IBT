FAQs

What is IBT all about?

IBT, or Internet-Based Test, is a method of conducting assessments online. It allows individuals to take tests remotely using a computer or digital device. IBT is used for various evaluations, covering academic, professional, and aptitude tests. This format is popular for its convenience, accessibility, and ability to reach a wide audience regardless of location. Test sections often include skill assessments like critical thinking and problem-solving. IBT serves as a versatile tool for evaluating competencies in different fields in today's digital age.

How does IBT benefit students?

IBT allows students to track their performance against international standards, opening doors to global opportunities.

Is IBT only for specific subjects?

IBT, or Internet-Based Test, is not limited to specific subjects. It is a versatile format used for various assessments across different fields such as mathematics, science, history, and computer skills. Its flexibility makes it suitable for evaluating competencies in diverse areas, making it a valuable tool for academic, professional, and aptitude testing.

Can anyone participate in IBT?

Yes, IBT is open to students worldwide, providing a platform for global comparison and growth.

How can educators get involved with IBT?

Educators can engage with Internet-Based Testing (IBT) by incorporating it into assessments for different subjects, promoting interactive learning, and enhancing digital literacy among students.
